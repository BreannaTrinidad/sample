Characters in Noli Me Tangere

    Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin

  • He is the son of Don Rafael Ibarra. His motherland is in the Philippines, while his second homeland is in Spain, Europe. Compared to the rest of the people in the party that Captain Tiago held up, Ibarra stood out the most in the crowd because of the way he was dressed.
    		•

    II. Don Santiago

  • Also known to most people as "Kapitan Tiago". He is a very welcoming and kind person, according to the people that live near his residence. Despite his being of a Filipino, his house resembles that of a rich Spaniard's due to the way he dresses and acts, as well as the house that he owns. His residence is always open for events or parties, and he welcomes anyone into his house, whether they are rich or not.
    		•

    III. Don Rafael Ibarra

  • He is known as Crisostomo Ibarra's father. He was a good friend of the Tenyente Heneral. He was accused of physically hurting an Artillery man after the Artillery's encounter with kids. Unfortunately, Don Rafael died in prison.
    		•

    IV. Padre Damaso

  • Father or Padre Damaso is a Fransiscan priest of the land of San Diego. He is an arrogant man who speaks hghly of himself and his opinions, because of his position as a priest. Sometimes, his opinions and the way he talks can lead him to a heated arguement with Padre Sibyla and the Tenyente Heneral.
    		•

    V. Tenyente Heneral

  • The Tenyente Heneral or the Lieutenant was one of the men who joined Kapitan Tiago's open party and sat together with Padre Sibyla and Padre Damaso. He was the one who sent Padre Damaso back into the Philippines from San Diego, even though Padre Damaso found no benefit in returning. As the Tenyente laid his eyes on Ibarra, he was reminded of his good friend, Don Rafael Ibarra.
